Designing the Museum of the Future

One week in Milan to design the museum that doesn't exist yet.

Fondazione Pini · Milan7–12 September 2026ACME × Fondazione Pini

what it is

Adolfo Pini was a scientist who chose to live surrounded by art. When he died, he left his house not to an heir, but to an idea: that beauty held in trust should return to those who come after. The building at Corso Garibaldi 2 today holds the works of the painter Bongiovanni Radice, an archive of documents and letters, and the upper studio-floors where painting, science and literature once shared the same rooms.

The question at the heart of the Summer School is a single one: what happens if this place — with its memory and its architecture — becomes the laboratory in which students invent the museum that does not yet exist?

Before any workshop, participants study Fondazione Pini: they walk it, measure it, read the letters in the archive, stand before the paintings. The Foundation's identity serves as brief, as material, as the constraint from which all the work descends. You design for a real problem, in a real space, with a real client.

2026 · sound

The 2026 edition is dedicated to Sound Design & Spatial Audio.

Sound is the first thing a museum doesn't show but that changes everything. This module explores how the sonic dimension — ambient composition, spatial audio, designed silence — transforms the perception of Fondazione Pini's spaces.

Participants listen before composing: the courtyard, the staircases, the rooms where Bongiovanni Radice painted. Then they build a sound proposal inseparable from that place — one that would make no sense anywhere else.

what you take home

Not an exercise. By the end of the week each participant takes home:

  • a vision document for a real institution;
  • a physical and digital mock-up of the proposed spaces and experiences;
  • a project assessed by the people who actually run that institution.

It's the kind of work that goes into a portfolio and opens real doors: the Summer School is also a moment where school, institution and the creative industry meet around a project, and the connections that form don't end with the week.

who it's for

The Summer School welcomes participants selected between the ages of 18 and 26.

The profile we look for is the disciplined curious: someone with a recognisable background — architecture, design, visual arts, music, communication, cultural studies — who feels the need to put it in relation with something beyond its own borders.

A share of international participants is expected, around 25–30% of the places.
